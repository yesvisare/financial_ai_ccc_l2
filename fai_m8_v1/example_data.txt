Apple Inc. Q1 FY2024 Financial Results

Apple reported EPS of $1.52, beating analyst expectations. The P/E ratio stands at 28,
indicating strong investor confidence. EBITDA increased 15% YoY, driven by improved
operational efficiency and margin expansion.

Valuation Analysis

Our DCF model uses a WACC of 8.5% based on current market conditions. The analysis
projects FCF growth of 12% over the next five years, with terminal value calculated
using the perpetuity growth method. The resulting NPV suggests the stock is fairly valued.

Balance Sheet Highlights

A/R increased by 20% QoQ, reflecting strong sales momentum. A/P remained stable,
indicating healthy supplier relationships. COGS decreased as a percentage of revenue,
contributing to improved gross margins. SG&A expenses were well-controlled at 15% of revenue.

Profitability Metrics

ROE improved to 45%, significantly above industry average. ROIC exceeded WACC by 8 percentage
points, demonstrating value creation. ROA of 18% reflects efficient asset utilization.
ROTA metrics show consistent improvement TTM.

M&A Activity and Market Trends

PE firms have been active in the technology sector, with several high-profile LBO transactions.
VC funding remains robust, supporting early-stage innovation. Recent IPO activity has been
strong, with multiple successful offerings. M&A multiples based on EV/EBITDA and EV/Sales
remain elevated.

Regulatory Compliance

The company maintains strict SOX compliance and follows GAAP standards in all financial
reporting. SEC filings are current and complete. FINRA regulations are observed for all
securities transactions. The company is preparing for MiFID II requirements for European operations.

Analyst Estimates

Q2 estimates project continued EPS growth with PEG ratio below 1.5, suggesting attractive
valuation relative to growth. P/B ratio of 12x reflects strong intangible asset value.
P/S ratio of 6x is in line with peer group averages.

Investment Thesis

The combination of strong FCF generation, disciplined capital allocation, and improving IRR
on new projects supports our positive outlook. CAPM-based required return of 10% is below
the expected return, creating a margin of safety for investors.
